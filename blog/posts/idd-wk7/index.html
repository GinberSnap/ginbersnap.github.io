IDD (Wk7): CLOs and MLOs

04-29-25

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

CLO1: By the end of the course, students will demonstrate proficiency in fundamental ethical hacking techniques and explain the role of ethical hacking in cybersecurity operations.

COL2: By the end of the course, students will evaluate how legal and regulatory frameworks shape the scope and implementation of ethical hacking and penetration testing.

CLO3: By the end of the course, students will apply foundational OSINT techniques to collect open-source intelligence, identify potential vulnerabilities, and recommend strategies to improve cybersecurity posture.

Module Learning Objectives (MLOs)

Module 1 (MLO1): Introduction to Ethical Hacking?
By the end of this module, students will be able to apply foundational ethical hacking techniques, and distinguish between ethical hacking and malicious hacking practices. Successful completion of MLO1 will enable learners to meet CLO1.

Module 2 (MLO2): Legal and ethical responsibilities
By the end of this module, students will evaluate legal and ethical challenges using a case study to demonstrate their understanding of fundamental principles and best practices. Successful completion of MLO2 will enable learners to meet CLO2.

Module 3 (MLO3): OSINT (Open Source Intelligent)
By the end of this module, students will be able to describe the role of OSINT in cybersecurity, utilize basic OSINT tools such as WHOIS and reverse image search, and analyze publicly available data to identify potential security insights. Successful completion of MLO3 will enable learners to meet CLO3.

Module 4 (MLO3): Hands-on exercise
By the end of this module, students will be able to execute basic OSINT reconnaissance, utilizing passive methods, to obtain publicly accessible intelligence. Successful completion of MLO4 will enable learners to meet CLO3.
